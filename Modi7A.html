Tamil Nadu rolling out red carpet for Modi-Xi meet in Mamallapuram

Mamallapuram in Tamil Nadu is all decked up ahead of the meeting of Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Chinese President Xi Jinping.

Mamallapuram is all set for a makeover ahead of the high-level summit between Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Chinese President Xi Jinping. On Friday past noon, the Chinese President will land at the Chennai airport and head off to a luxury hotel.

He will then proceed to meet Prime Minister Narendra Modi for tea. At 5pm, PM Modi and China's Xi will visit the four iconic monuments of Mamallapuram.

They will visit Arjuna's penance, Krishna's butter ball, Panchratha and the shore temple. All the monuments have been cleaned. Wooden walkways have been set up for the leaders to enjoy the heritage sites.

Natural grass lawns have been used in the landscaping. Lights have been installed as well to add to the photo opportunities there.

A Buddha statue has been installed near the shore temple to add to the cultural exchange between India and China.

After the leaders visit the monuments, a cultural programme will be conducted at the shore temple. This will be followed by dinner at Fishermen's Cove where Prime Minister Narendra Modi is staying.

On Sunday, the leaders will start their day with an informal meeting followed by lunch. Trade, terrorism, and border issues are topics that the two leaders are likely to discuss.

However, tourists have been left disappointed because movement has been restricted at the monuments till the completion of the VVIP visit. Tourists can admire the monuments only from outside.

"We are from Kolkata. We went to Puducherry and then came here but we haven't been allowed to see anything. This is disappointing. They should have put up notices online at least wecould have then planned accordingly," said Sharmista, a tourist.

As President Xi is to take the Old Mahabalipuram Road and the East Coast Road to reach Mamallapuram, the entire route has received a makeover.

The medians have been painted, new sign boards and reflectors have been erected, over-grown shrubbery and branches have been cut, and earth movers have been used to remove thick shrubbery. CCTV cameras have also been installed along the way. The road leading to the Fishermen's Cove also has new pavements.

The Agenda

In the Mamallapuram summit, the focus of the talks is likely to be on steps to ensure mutual development and expanding overall ties. The focus of the meeting is on improving people to people contact and on how to maintain peace and tranquillity on the India-China border.

PM Modi will hold one-to-one and delegation level talks with the Chinese President. The meeting between them has no specific agenda but issues like terrorism, terror funding, support and sourcing are likely to come up during the meet.

The two leaders are likely to decide dates for the next Special Representatives during the informal meeting in Tamil Nadu later this week.
